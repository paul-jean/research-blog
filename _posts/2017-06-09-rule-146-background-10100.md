Figure: Comparison of behavior of rule 146 on two different repetitive backgrounds: a simple background of 0's (a and c), and a background consisting of repetitions of the block 01001 (b and d).  On the block backgrounds (b) and (d), there are have been placed a slightly different block 01101 at two positions.  When these 01101 blocks are placed an odd number of blocks apart, as in (b), they superimpose in the same way that the single black cells do in (a).  When they are an even number of blocks apart, they interfere, similar to the same situation with single black cells in (c).  This behavior suggests that these 01001 blocks are emulating the role of 0's, and the 01101 blocks emulating the role of 1's.  These blocks were found by enumerating repetitive backgrounds in rule 146 and considering point perturbations to the backgrounds (note that blocks 01101 and 01001 differ only by one bit).
